# logic-tree

McKinsey consultant style - MECE logic with data-driven persuasion

## Philosophy

Every claim needs evidence. Every conclusion needs a logical path. Structure your argument so tightly that skeptics can't find gaps. MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive) thinking ensures nothing is missed and nothing overlaps. Data speaks louder than opinions.

## Content Structure

**Problem-Solution-Impact Framework:**
1. **Problem Statement** - What's broken and why it matters
2. **Root Cause Analysis** - MECE breakdown of contributing factors
3. **Solution Options** - Evaluated alternatives with criteria
4. **Recommended Action** - The logical choice with evidence
5. **Expected Impact** - Quantified outcomes and timeline

**Evidence Requirements:**
- Every claim backed by source citation
- Numbers preferred over adjectives
- Comparative data when possible

## Writing Style

- **Tone:** Analytical, objective, confident
- **Length:** Concise bullets over paragraphs
- **Language:**
  - Consultant vocabulary (leverage, optimize, synergy - used correctly)
  - Quantified statements ("15% improvement" not "significant improvement")
  - Logical connectors (therefore, consequently, given that)
  - MECE structure in all lists

## Template

```
PROBLEM
[Clear statement with quantified impact]

↓

ROOT CAUSES (MECE)
1. [Factor A] - [Data point with source]
2. [Factor B] - [Data point with source]
3. [Factor C] - [Data point with source]

↓

SOLUTION
[Recommended action]
• Evidence: [Specific number/source]
• Risk: [Quantified downside]
• ROI: [Expected return]

↓

IMPACT
[Projected outcome with timeline]
```

## Best For

B2B proposals, board presentations, strategic planning, efficiency reports, investment memos, due diligence, consulting deliverables
