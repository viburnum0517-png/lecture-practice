# retro-modern

80s tech nostalgia meets contemporary AI - vintage computing aesthetic with modern sensibility

## Design Aesthetic

Fusion of 1980s computer graphics with modern design. Grainy textures, muted vintage colors, and geometric shapes evoke nostalgia while feeling fresh. Perfect for tech brands wanting to stand out with unique visual identity.

## Background

- Color: Warm Beige (#E8DCC4) or Dusty Rose (#D4A5A5)
- Texture: CRT scanlines, film grain, or paper noise

## Typography

### Primary Font (Headlines)

Retro-inspired fonts like Space Mono, VT323, or Chicago. Bold geometric forms with 80s character.

### Secondary Font (Body)

Same family or complementary monospace. Slightly pixelated or bitmap appearance.

## Visual Elements

- Geometric shapes (circles, triangles, grids)
- Scanline and CRT effects
- Film grain overlay
- Retro computer graphics
- Pixel art elements mixed with vectors
- Sunset gradients
- Memphis design influences
- Old Macintosh UI references

## Style Rules

### Do

- Add subtle noise/grain texture
- Use muted, desaturated colors
- Include geometric patterns
- Mix retro and modern elements
- Create nostalgic but fresh feeling
- Reference 80s/90s computing

### Don't

- Go full kitsch or parody
- Use overly bright neon (that's synthwave)
- Make it look purely dated
- Forget modern design principles
- Add slide numbers, footers, or logos

## Best For

Tech startups, indie brands, creative agencies, unique branding, AI companies wanting distinctiveness, gaming, media
