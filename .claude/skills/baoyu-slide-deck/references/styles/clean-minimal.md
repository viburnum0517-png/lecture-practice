# clean-minimal

Apple-inspired ultra-clean aesthetics with vast negative space and refined simplicity

## Design Aesthetic

The art of reduction taken to its extreme. Every element must justify its existence. Inspired by Apple's design philosophy - clean lines, generous whitespace, and a single focal point per slide. Premium, sophisticated, and effortlessly elegant.

## Background

- Color: Pure White (#FFFFFF)
- Texture: None - absolute pristine cleanliness

## Typography

### Primary Font (Headlines)

Apple-style SF Pro Display, Helvetica Neue, or Inter. Thin to light weight for elegance. Generous tracking (letter-spacing). Large scale creates impact without boldness.

### Secondary Font (Body)

Same family in lighter weight. Minimal size contrast. Everything breathes with space.

## Color Palette

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Background | Pure White | #FFFFFF | Everything |
| Primary Text | Near Black | #1D1D1F | Headlines only |
| Secondary Text | Gray | #86868B | Body, captions |
| Accent | Apple Blue | #0071E3 | Single accent, very sparingly |
| Dividers | Ultra Light Gray | #F5F5F7 | Subtle separators |

## Visual Elements

- Single product/concept per slide
- Thin hairline dividers (0.5px)
- Generous margins (20%+ on all sides)
- Center-aligned or asymmetric balance
- Simple icons (SF Symbols style)
- Subtle gradients on product images only
- No decorative elements whatsoever
- Photography with perfect lighting and shadows

## Style Rules

### Do

- Embrace emptiness as design
- Limit to one accent color per deck
- Keep text to 5 words or less when possible
- Create dramatic scale differences
- Let content breathe with whitespace
- Use product photography as hero elements

### Don't

- Fill empty space with anything
- Use multiple colors or gradients
- Add icons unless absolutely necessary
- Create busy layouts
- Use stock photography
- Add slide numbers, footers, or logos

## Best For

Apple-style keynotes, luxury products, premium services, executive presentations, minimalist brands, design portfolios, product launches
